# Bubble Bank (Live Night Lines)

> **Status:** content canon. **Documentation only.** Reusable short
> bubble lines for live-night surfaces — staff, guests, regulars.
> Voice copy that goes **over** a visual cue (per
> [`UIUX_Reference_Pack.md`](UIUX_Reference_Pack.md) §16 Rule 4 / §17).
>
> **Rules for these lines:**
>
> - **Short.** Under ~6 words. Floor-first; the bubble is the cherry
>   on the visual, never the whole signal.
> - **Spoken-sounding**, not management-report. Per UIUX §10 copy
>   tone and `nightclub-content-writing` skill.
> - **No numbers.** No "service ratio." Voices, not metrics.
> - **In-character.** Use voice notes from
>   [`character-bible.md`](character-bible.md). Caramel: "bro." John:
>   barks. Rosa: warm + dry. Grace: by-the-book.
> - **Cast rule.** Current characters can speak now. Future
>   characters (Ayan, Janer, Elfen, Kerem, DJs, waiters, dancers,
>   VIPs) tagged `(future)`.
> - **Bubble timing** — sub-second hold, max 2–3 active on screen at
>   once. Per UIUX §17 Pacing of communication.
>
> Use these as **pools** — the engine picks one at random per beat,
> not a fixed script.

## Authoring rules (when adding new bubble lines)

1. **Pool, not script.** Multiple lines per pressure state so the
   engine can rotate without repetition fatigue.
2. **Voice match.** Caramel never says "boss" without "bro." John
   never softens. Rosa never gushes. (Per `character-bible.md`.)
3. **Pressure → visual → bubble.** Per UIUX §16 Rule 1. The bubble
   never *introduces* a problem; the floor already showed it.
4. **No graphic content.** No fight detail, no sexual content, no
   identity slurs, no real brands. Per `nightclub-safety-framing`.
5. **No promises the room can't keep.** If a line implies a system
   that doesn't exist yet (e.g. a comp, a refund, a callback), it's
   FUTURE — tag it.
6. **Localizability.** Lines should translate without losing voice.
   Avoid idioms that don't carry.
