## Tone — Neon Noir

Glamorous and seductive on the surface; risk, consequence, and survival
underneath. Inspired by the *emotional feel* of Nightclub City — deeper, darker,
original, offline-first. Mood words: neon, late-night, magnetic,
dangerous-but-controllable, earned glory.

## Premise

Club Empire is a game about **a group of people trying to build a nightlife
empire together.** You take a run-down basement club and turn it into the best
night in the city — one risky, neon-soaked night at a time. The real difference
maker is the **friend group, staff chemistry, and relationship pressure**, with
the owner caught between **chaos and discipline.**

## The central tension — Party vs Empire

The emotional spine (see `gameplay-north-star.md`):

- **Ayan — Party Side:** chaos, hype, ambition, legendary nights, risk.
- **Kerem — Empire Side:** discipline, structure, survival, staff development.
- **The best club needs both.**

The defining theme:

> Can you build a nightclub empire without letting your hunger for legendary
> nights destroy the system holding it together?

### The three voices of a decision (lore framing)

- **Ayan:** "Bro, let's do it."
- **Kerem:** "Bro, think first."
- **Elfen:** "Bro, what happens *after*?"

## Bridge figures

- **Caramel** — understands the madness but respects structure; the human bridge
  between Ayan-chaos and Kerem-discipline. (Character canon; see character bible.)
- **Elfen** — the conscience/"what happens after" voice; sees consequences before
  others do. (Future recurring character, not staff.)

## World facts established by the current build

- The club grows by reputation tiers: Nobody's Club → Local Spot → Rising Name →
  City Favorite → Best in the City.
- Nights have texture: a crowd, a bar that can fall behind, a door that can lose
  control, an energy that can cool — the beats the player feels.

## Memorable-moments belief

Success should be measured in remembered people and moments, not just numbers:
"I should have listened to Elfen." · "Ayan talked me into it again." · "John
nearly got us fined." · "Caramel saved the night."

## Future recurring character — Elfen — **DO NOT BUILD YET**

> Narrative role here; full profile in `character-bible.md`. **Not staff, not
> hireable.** Implementation status: **FUTURE RECURRING CHARACTER / DO NOT BUILD YET.**

Elfen is a **friend of the club / regular patron** — the **conscience of the
friend group.** She is *not* a saint, not a lecturer, not a moral-police figure.
She understands nightlife, partying, temptation, and consequences, and gives
**sincere observations, not stat lectures.**

Core line:
> If Ayan says "Bro let's do it," and Kerem says "Bro think first," Elfen says
> "Bro what happens *after*?"

She **never reveals hidden numbers.** She comments on **visible social shifts:**
- "Your staff look exhausted."
- "People seem uncomfortable tonight."
- "The atmosphere feels different lately."
- "Your regulars don't seem as happy as before."

Painful disappointment line (if the club loses its way):
> "I know this isn't what you wanted this place to be."
