# Nightclub Safety Framing

Club Empire is an edgy nightclub tycoon. The edge stays in **satire and business
risk**, never in real-world instruction. This skill is the tone-and-content
guardrail for anything that could read as "shady".

## The four framings

- **Mechanics, not methods.** Model the *system* (a chance, a cost, a reputation
  hit) — never a how-to. "Relaxed smoking raises compliance risk" is a dial;
  describing how to evade an inspection is not allowed.
- **Manage exposure, not behavior.** The player tunes risk exposure and lives with
  consequences; they are not coached to do anything illegal in the real world.
- **Business / reputation / compliance consequences.** Downsides are fines,
  reputation loss, lost revenue, incidents handled abstractly — the language of
  running a venue, not of crime.
- **No glamorized violence.** Incidents are abstract ("security handled it, it cost
  you"), never detailed, gory, or celebrated.

## How current systems are framed (use as the model)

- **Relaxed smoking →** a compliance gamble: more crowd now vs. an inspector fine +
  reputation hit. Satire, not evasion advice.
- **Theft (dishonest bartender) →** "shrinkage" / skimming, surfaced as a loss line.
- **Incidents (thin security) →** abstract scuffles handled by the door.

## When to use

- Designing or naming a risk/compliance mechanic, or writing copy about one.
- Reviewing whether an edgy idea or line crosses into instruction or glamorization.

## When NOT to use

- Neutral systems with no risk/illicit dimension (pricing, capacity, plain staffing).

## Hard boundaries

- **No real-world illegal instructions or how-to**, ever (drugs, weapons, fraud,
  tax/laundering methods, evasion techniques, etc.).
- **No real people, venues, or brands.** Fictional archetypes only.
- **No glamorized or graphic violence**; keep incidents abstract.
- If a requested mechanic can't be expressed as abstract exposure/consequence,
  push back and reframe it — or decline — rather than implement it.
